Lion Scientific Name: Panthera leo Habitat: Grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and parts of India. Diet: Carnivore (feeds on large prey like zebras, antelopes, and buffalo). Social Structure: Lions are social animals and live in groups called prides, typically consisting of related females, their offspring, and a few males. Distinct Features: Male lions have prominent manes; they are apex predators known for their strength and roar.

Wolf Scientific Name: Canis lupus Habitat: Diverse, ranging from tundra and forests to deserts and grasslands, primarily in the Northern Hemisphere. Diet: Carnivore (feeds on deer, elk, and smaller mammals). Social Structure: Highly social animals that live in packs with a defined hierarchy. Distinct Features: Known for their sharp instincts, strong hunting skills, and ability to adapt to various environments.

Tiger Scientific Name: Panthera tigris Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and mangroves in Asia. Diet: Carnivore (feeds on large mammals like deer and wild boar). Social Structure: Solitary animals except during mating or when females are raising cubs. Distinct Features: Largest of the big cats; recognized by their orange coat with black stripes.

Cat Scientific Name: Felis catus Habitat: Domesticated; found worldwide in human homes and feral populations in urban and rural areas. Diet: Carnivore (prefers meat-based diets, though some are fed commercial cat food). Social Structure: Independent but can form bonds with humans and other animals. Distinct Features: Known for agility, keen hunting instincts, and playful behavior.

Dog Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris Habitat: Domesticated; found worldwide in a variety of living environments. Diet: Omnivore (primarily fed commercial dog food, though they can eat a variety of foods). Social Structure: Highly social animals that bond strongly with humans and other animals. Distinct Features: Known for loyalty, intelligence, and ability to be trained for tasks like herding, hunting, or companionship.
