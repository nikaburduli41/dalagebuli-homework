Dog (Canis lupus familiaris): Dogs are domesticated mammals and are one of the earliest animals to be domesticated by humans. They are known for their loyalty, intelligence, and companionship. Dogs come in various breeds, each with distinct characteristics and temperaments.

Lion (Panthera leo): Lions are large carnivorous felines native to Africa and parts of Asia. They are unique among big cats for their social structure, living in groups called prides. Male lions are distinguished by their prominent manes. Lions are apex predators, playing a crucial role in their ecosystem.

Cat (Felis catus): Domestic cats are small, carnivorous mammals that have been companions to humans for thousands of years. They are known for their agility, sharp retractable claws, and keen hunting instincts. Cats are popular pets worldwide, valued for their companionship and ability to control pests.

Tiger (Panthera tigris): Tigers are the largest species among the big cats, recognizable by their distinctive orange coats with black stripes. They are solitary and territorial animals found primarily in Asia. Tigers are apex predators, primarily hunting large ungulates. Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect tigers, as they are listed as endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Parrot (Order: Psittaciformes): Parrots are a diverse group of birds known for their vibrant plumage and strong, curved beaks. They are highly intelligent and are capable of mimicking human speech. Parrots are found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. Many species are kept as pets, but some face threats from habitat loss and the pet trade.
